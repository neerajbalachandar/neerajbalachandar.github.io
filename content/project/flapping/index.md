This project explores low-fidelity vortex particle methods (VPM) to model chaotic wakes generated by flapping-wing motion. The objective is to preserve the essential wake physics while keeping the computational cost manageable for parametric studies and control-oriented models.

## Research Questions
- Which wake features are necessary to retain predictive accuracy in low-fidelity models?
- How does fidelity affect the stability of the predicted wake structures?

## Approach
- Vortex particle discretization with tunable fidelity
- Comparison across operating regimes to assess accuracy and stability
- Extraction of wake descriptors for reduced-order modeling

## Status
Archived. The framework is stable and serves as a baseline for future reduced-order studies.

## Tools
Julia/Python, numerical methods for vortex dynamics.
